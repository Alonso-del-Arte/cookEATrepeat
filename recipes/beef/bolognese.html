Beef! It's whats for dinner!

Algorithm:

  1. In a medium, heavy-bottom pot, heat 2 tablespoons canola oil over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the onion, carrots, celery, garlic and thyme and season with salt.
  3. Cook, stirring regularly using a wooden spoon, until the vegetables are tender.
  4. Add the beef, veal and pork, stirring and breaking up over high heat.
  5. Season with salt and several grinds pepper, to taste.
  6. Continue to cook over high heat until the meat is brown, stirring regularly, keep the meat and vegetables from scorching.
  7. Once the meat is browned, stir in the tomato paste over medium heat. Cook for about 20 minutes, stirring regularly, to marry the flavors.
  8. Add the wine, bring to a boil and cook until the wine is reduced by half.
  9. Add the milk and bay leaves and bring to a boil.
  10. Reduce the heat to a simmer and cook for 2 hours, stirring occasionally.
  11. You may need to add a bit of water (or chicken or veal stock, if you have it) to thin the sauce if it thickens too much.
  12. Taste the sauce and season with salt and several grinds of pepper.
  13. Remove from heat and stir to release the steam and allow it to cool slightly.

Variables:

  • 1 large yellow onion, finely chopped
  • 3 large carrots, finely chopped
  • 3 ribs celery, finely chopped
  • 1 tomato peeled, no seeds, diced
  • 5 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • 6 sprigs fresh thyme
  • salt and pepper
  • 1 1/2 pound ground beef chuck
  • 1/2 pound ground veal
  • 1/2 pound ground pork
  • 6 ounces tomato paste (maybe more)
  • 1 cup Lambrusco (Fortissimo, Paisano, etc.)
  • 1 1/2 cups milk
  • 2 bay leaves

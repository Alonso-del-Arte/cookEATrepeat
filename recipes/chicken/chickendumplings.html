Winner! Winner! Chicken Dinner!

Algorithm:

  1. Cut chuck roast into 3" cubes, trim large chunks of fat. Sprinkle generously with salt and let rest at room temperature.
  2. Heat canola oil till shimmering in frying pan to sear beef in.
  3. Sear all sides of beef till crispy edges form and a nice seared brown exterior is on at least half the pieces. Add oil as needed.
  4. When immediatly finished searing, deglaze the pan with the onions and 1/2 cup of beef stock.
  5. Scrape pan for fried bits and fat remnants.
  6. Dump contents of frying pan into slow cooker with remaining onions, thyme, rosemary, garlic, 1/2 the oregano and bay leafs.
  7. Place beef atop, cover with remaining oregano. Pour beef stock, leaving a bit of the beef exposed.
  8. Cover, and cook on Low for 10 to 12 hours. Beef will shred with a fork.
  9. Shred with fork.
  10. Serve in a hoagie with mustard and peppers.

Variables:

  • 3 large carrots, chopped 1/4"
  • 1 cup green peas
  • 3 stalks celery, chopped 1/4"
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1/4 cup flour

Dumplings:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 4 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon pepper
  • 1 large egg, beaten
  • 2 tablespoons butter, melted
  • 3/4 to 1 cup whole milk
  • 1/4 cup parsley, diced fine

  • Dumplings: combine dry ingredients and parsley in a medium bowl.
  • Stir in egg, butter and enough milk to make a moist stiff batter.
  • Use Chicken Stock Recipe. Alternatively, use a rotiserie chicken.
  • Shred or cube chicken.
  • Fill stock pot with broth, reserve 1/2 cup for the roux. Add vegetables and bay leaf. Bring to a boil and simmmer till carrots are tender.
  • Add 1/4 cup flour to reserved stock, whisk till silky, slowly add to boiling stock while stirring with purpose.
  • Return chicken to stockpot. Bring to a gentle boil.
  • Drop dumplings in by teaspoonfuls into soup. Cover and cook without lifting the lid for 18-20 minutes.
  • Serve.

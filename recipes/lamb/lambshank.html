Flavor so good it's criminal... on the Lamb

Algorithm:

  1. Preheat an oven to 275°F
  2. Rinse and dry Lamb Shanks.
  3. Sprinkle liberally with salt all over.
  4. Braise in hot canola oil and set aside.
  5. Pour the water into the hot pan and use a wooden spatula to help scrape off any caramelized bits from the bottom of the pan.
  6. When almost all of the liquid has evaporated, add the onions, carrots, leeks, garlic, herbs, 1 Tbs. canola oil and 1 tsp. kosher salt.
  7. Stir the vegetables frequently until they just begin to become translucent.
  8. Add the red wine and increase the heat to high. Boil the wine until you can no longer smell the alcohol, then reduce the heat to a simmer.
  9. Cover the pan with the lid. Return the liquid to a simmer, then transfer the pan to the center of the oven.
  10. Bake @3 hours, till they are almost falling from the bone.
  11. Reduce the oven temperature to its lowest setting or about 150°F. Place the shanks back in the warm oven.
  12. Strain the vegetables and liquid from the pan through a coarse-mesh strainer and collect them in a bowl or pot. Discard the vegetables.
  13. Place the pan over medium heat, add the vinegar and bring the liquid to a boil. Continue to reduce the liquid to a slightly syrupy sauce consistency

Variables:

  • 4 medium lamb shanks, each 1 1/4 to 1 1/2 lb.
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground pepper
  • 1/2 cup water or low-salt chicken stock
  • 3 large onions, cut into 3/8-inch dice
  • 4 large carrots, cut into 3/8-inch dice
  • 2 large leeks, cut into 3/8-inch dice and rinsed well
  • 4 large garlic cloves, peeled and lightly crushed
  • 6 thyme sprigs
  • 6 parsley stems
  • 2 fresh bay leaves
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 1 Tbs. red wine vinegar

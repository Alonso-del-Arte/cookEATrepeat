Everything is better when it is wetter.
Straight from the sea!

Algorithm:

  1. Fry the onion in the oil in a 16-inch paella pan until soft, stirring often.
  2. Stir in the garlic, and before it begins to color, add the green/red peppers.
  3. Add the carrots, celery, salt to taste, paprika, chilli powder, pepper flakes.
  4. Add chorizo, cook till crumbled and browned.
  5. Add chicken, cook till browned.
  6. Add tomatoes and green peas.
  7. Add the rice and stir well until all the grains are coated. Sizzle the rice in the oil for a minute.
  8. Pour stock over rice and bring to a boil, add saffron and salt to taste.
  9. Stir well and spread the rice out evenly in the pan (DO NOT stir again).
  10. Cook the rice over low heat for 20-30 minutes, moving the pan around and rotating it so that the rice cooks evenly.
  11. Lay the shrimp and fish on top with @5mins left. Stock level should be just showing the top layer of rice at this point. Cover with foil.
  12. Add mussels, clams and a touch of stock, if needed, when rice is about done. Cover with foil and steam.
  13. As soon as the mussels open, they are cooked. Throw away any that have not opened.
  14. Rice should have a nice brown carmelization on the edges with just about no stock remaining.

Variables:

  • 1 large spanish onion, finely chopped
  • 5 tablespoons canola oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, pressed
  • 1 green pepper
  • 1/2 cup green peas
  • 1 carrot, chopped 1/4" pieces
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 pound ground chorizo
  • 1 pound chicken thighs, boneless and 1/2" cubes
  • 8 oz cut of fish (Halibut, Sea Bass, etc.)
  • 8 clams
  • 8 mussels
  • 12 jumbo shrimp in their shells
  • 1 teaspoon spanish paprika
  • A good pinch of saffron threads
  • 2 cups medium-grain Calaspara rice
  • 3 cups fish or chicken stock, plus more if needed
  • salt and pepper
